# Art Robbery Rules

## Overview

You are a gang of thieves who just robbed an art museum. The heist was easy — **sharing the loot** is the real challenge. Over four raids, claim loot tiles by playing cards, steal from rivals, collect **Alibis**, and end with the richest haul. Designed by **Reiner Knizia**, published by Helvetiq.

**Goal:** After four raids, have the most loot points — but don't finish with the fewest Alibis, or the police will catch you!

## Components

- **55 cards**
  - 36 Number cards (0–5)
  - 6 Boss cards
  - 6 Guard Dog cards
  - 7 Greedy Thief cards
- **36 Loot tiles** (9 tiles × 4 raids: Sketches, Sculptures, Antiquities, Paintings)
- **1 Guard Dog figure**
- Rulebook

## Setup

1. Sort Loot tiles into **four stacks** by back design (one stack per raid).
2. Place the **Guard Dog figure** in the middle.
3. Shuffle all cards. Deal **5 cards** to each player. Remaining cards form a face-down draw pile.

## How to Play

The game has **four raids**. At the start of each raid, place that raid's 9 Loot tiles face up in the middle.

| Raid | Collection |
|:----:|:-----------|
| 1 | Sketches |
| 2 | Sculptures |
| 3 | Antiquities |
| 4 | Paintings |

The Guard Dog figure starts in the middle only during **Raid 1**. The player who most recently visited a museum starts Raid 1; play continues clockwise.

### On Your Turn

1. Play **one card** from your hand face up onto the discard pile.
2. Take the matching Loot tile (if possible) and place it face up in front of you.
3. Draw the top card of the draw pile to refill your hand to **5 cards**.

If the draw pile runs out, reshuffle the discard pile into a new draw pile. If you play a card that cannot take any tile, nothing else happens.

### Card Effects

| Card | Effect |
|------|--------|
| **Number (0–5)** | Take a Loot tile of that value from the middle. If several match, take only one. If none remain in the middle, **steal** a tile of that value from another player. |
| **Guard Dog** | Take the Guard Dog figure (from wherever it is). |
| **Greedy Thief** | Take **any one** tile from the middle. Cannot steal from players. |
| **Boss** | Take the Boss tile for this raid. |

### Guard Dog Protection

If you try to steal a tile from a player who has the Guard Dog figure, **they choose**: give you the tile, **or** give you the Guard Dog figure instead (keeping their tile).

### Alibis

Some Loot tiles (especially value **0**) show white dots — **Alibis**. Each white dot counts as one Alibi. Collect as many as you can; the player with the **fewest** Alibis at game end is in trouble.

## End of a Raid

A raid ends when the **last tile** is taken from the middle.

1. **Boss check:** You may keep the Boss tile only if you also have a **4 or 5** tile from the **same raid**. Otherwise return the Boss tile to the box.
2. All players stack their Loot tiles **face down** in front of them. These tiles are safe and can no longer be stolen.
3. Set out the next raid's tiles face up in the middle.
4. Keep your hand of cards; **do not** reshuffle between raids.
5. The player with the Guard Dog figure starts the next raid.

## End of the Game

After the fourth raid:

### 1. Alibis

- **3–5 players:** The player(s) with the fewest Alibis are **arrested** (eliminated; score 0). Ties: all lowest are eliminated.
- **2 players:** The player with fewer Alibis is not eliminated, but must discard tiles totaling **10** points.

### 2. Loot

Sum the values of remaining Loot tiles. Each **Boss** tile is worth **5** points. Highest total wins. On a tie, the tied player with the **most Alibis** wins.

## Tips

- Value **0** tiles are weak loot but often carry Alibis — useful insurance against arrest.
- The Boss is high value but worthless without a 4 or 5 from that raid.
- Holding the Guard Dog slows thieves targeting your tiles.
