# Bomb Busters Rules

## Overview

Bomb Busters is a cooperative deduction game for 2–5 players designed by Hisashi Hayashi. Players form a bomb disposal squad, working together to cut matching wires and defuse a bomb before it explodes. The game features 66 progressive missions that introduce new rules and equipment, creating a campaign-style experience.

## Components

- 1 game board with detonator dial
- 48 blue wire tiles (numbers 1–12, 4 of each)
- 11 yellow wire tiles ("1.1" to "11.1")
- 11 red wire tiles ("1.5" to "11.5")
- 5 tile stands
- 5 character cards with unique abilities
- 12 equipment cards
- 26 info tokens (including 2 yellow)
- 12 validation tokens
- 8 mission cards (plus surprise boxes for later missions)
- 7 markers (4 yellow, 3 red)
- 1 Bomb Busters standee

## Setup

1. **Choose a mission.** Missions get progressively harder; it's recommended to play in order.
2. **Designate a Captain** (random for the first mission, then rotate left). The Captain reads the mission card aloud.
3. **Distribute tile stands** based on player count:

| Players | Captain Stands | Others' Stands |
|:-------:|:--------------:|:--------------:|
| 2       | 2              | 2              |
| 3       | 2              | 1              |
| 4       | 1              | 1              |
| 5       | 1              | 1              |

4. **Prepare wire tiles:** Collect 48 blue wires, add random red and yellow wires as indicated by the mission card. Place colored markers on the board to indicate which red/yellow values are in play.
5. **Deal wires** face-down equally among all tile stands.
6. **Sort wires** on stands from left to right in ascending order (face toward owner). The decimal values on red/yellow wires are only for sorting.
7. **Place equipment cards** face-up on the board (unusable until unlocked).
8. **Place info tokens:** Starting with the Captain, each player places one info token pointing at one of their blue wires with a matching value to give the team a starting clue.

## How to Play

Starting with the Captain, players take turns clockwise. On your turn, you **must** perform one of three actions:

### Dual Cut (Main Action)

The core action of the game. You attempt to cut two wires of the same value — one yours, one a teammate's.

1. Point at a specific wire on a teammate's stand.
2. State a value (e.g., "This wire is a 9").
3. Resolve:
   - **Correct:** Both wires are revealed and placed face-up in front of their stands — successfully cut!
   - **Wrong (blue/yellow wire):** The detonator advances 1 space. The teammate places an info token showing the wire's true value.
   - **Wrong (red wire):** The bomb **explodes immediately** — mission failed!

**Note:** You do not reveal where in your hand the matching wire is.

### Solo Cut

If the remaining wires of a certain value are **all** in your hand, you can cut them without involving a teammate.

- If you have all 4 wires of the same value → cut all 4 at once.
- If 2 wires of that value were already cut and you hold the remaining 2 → cut both.

Solo cuts are based on public information and cannot fail.

### Reveal Your Red Wires

If **all** your remaining uncut wires are red, reveal them. This removes you from play — your turns are skipped going forward.

## Special Wires

### Yellow Wires

Yellow wires are cut the same way as blue wires, but during gameplay they all share the value **"YELLOW"** (the printed number is only for sorting during setup).

- To cut a yellow wire via Dual Cut: say "This wire is yellow."
- Solo Cut with yellow wires requires having all remaining yellows in your hand.

### Red Wires

Red wires are deadly. If a Dual Cut targets a red wire incorrectly, the bomb explodes. Red wires share the value **"RED"** during gameplay.

## Equipment & Characters

- **Equipment cards** unlock when 2 wires of the value shown on the card have been cut. Most equipment can be used by anyone at any time (once per mission).
- **Character cards** give each player a unique one-time ability (e.g., the Double Detector lets you point at 2 wires during a Dual Cut).

## Validation Tokens

When all 4 wires of a value have been cut, place a validation token on that number on the board as a reminder.

## Communication Rules

- **Forbidden:** Discussing wire values, implying values, recalling info from previous turns, or sharing guesses aloud.
- **Allowed:** Discussing general tactics, equipment usage, and reminding teammates about available abilities.

## Game End

- **Victory:** All tile stands are empty — every wire has been cut!
- **Defeat:** The bomb explodes (a red wire is cut, or the detonator reaches the skull space).

If a mission fails, change the Captain and try again.

## Campaign Progression

Complete mission 8 to unlock the first surprise box (missions 9–19). Each box introduces new rules, equipment, and challenges. Track your progress on the box's score sheet.
