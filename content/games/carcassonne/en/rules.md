# Carcassonne Rules

## Overview

Carcassonne is a classic tile-laying game set in the medieval French countryside. Players take turns drawing and placing tiles to build cities, roads, monasteries, and fields. Players deploy their followers (meeples) to claim features and score points when they are completed. The player with the most points wins.

## Components

- **72 land tiles** — Featuring cities, roads, monasteries, and fields
- **40 followers (meeples)** — 8 in each of 5 colors
- **1 scoreboard**
- **5 abbots** (1 per color, for gardens)

## Setup

1. Place the starting tile (dark back) in the center of the table.
2. Shuffle the remaining tiles and place face down as the draw pile.
3. Each player takes 8 meeples of their chosen color (7 followers + 1 abbot).
4. Place one meeple of each color on the scoreboard (0).

## How to Play

On your turn, you must perform the following actions in order:

### 1. Draw and Place a Tile
Draw one tile and place it adjacent to existing tiles. The new tile must match all adjacent edges:
- Roads must connect to roads
- City walls must connect to city walls
- Fields must connect to fields

If a tile cannot be legally placed anywhere, it is discarded and a new tile is drawn.

### 2. Deploy a Follower (Optional)
After placing your tile, you may place one meeple on that tile on one of these features:
- **Road** — As a Highwayman
- **City** — As a Knight
- **Monastery** — As a Monk
- **Field** — As a Farmer (laid on its side)

You CANNOT place a meeple on a feature already claimed by another player, even on a different tile. However, features can become shared if two separate claimed features are later connected.

### 3. Score Completed Features
If the tile you placed completes a feature, score it immediately and return any meeples on it:

| Feature | Scoring |
|---------|---------|
| **Road** | 1 point per tile in the completed road |
| **City** | 2 points per tile + 2 points per coat of arms (pennant/shield) |
| **Monastery** | 1 point per surrounding tile + 1 for the monastery itself (max 9 points) |

Ties for cities and roads: the player with the most meeples on the feature scores all points. If tied, all tied players score full points.

## End Game & Final Scoring

When the last tile is placed, the game ends. Uncompleted features score partial points:

| Feature | Final Scoring |
|---------|--------------|
| **Road** | 1 point per tile |
| **City** | 1 point per tile + 1 point per coat of arms |
| **Monastery** | 1 point per surrounding tile + monastery |
| **Field** | 3 points per completed city adjacent to the field |

## Farmer Scoring

Farmers are never returned during the game. At the end, each field supplies each completed city it touches. Each city scores 3 points for **each field** that connects to it. The player(s) with the most farmers in each field earn those points.

## The Abbot

The abbot can only be placed on a monastery or garden. At the start of any turn, you may choose to **retrieve your abbot** instead of placing a tile. The abbot's monastery/ garden scores immediately as if it were the end of the game, and you forfeit your tile placement that turn.

## Winning

The player with the highest total score after final scoring wins.
