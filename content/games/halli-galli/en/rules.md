# Halli Galli Rules

## Overview

**Halli Galli** (Chinese edition: **哈铃果铃**) is a lightning-fast reaction game by **Haim Shafir**, published by Amigo. Flip fruit cards in turn; the moment the face-up cards show **exactly five** of the same fruit, race to ring the bell and claim the piles.

**Goal:** Hold the **most cards** when the game ends.

## Components

- **56 fruit cards** (strawberries, bananas, limes, plums — each card shows 1–5 fruits)
- **1 bell**
- Instructions

## Setup

1. Place the **bell** in the centre of the table (use a cloth or felt to protect the surface).
2. Shuffle all cards and deal them **face down**, one at a time, until none remain. Hands need not be equal.
3. Each player forms a face-down draw pile in front of them. Do not look at the cards.

## How to Play

The player to the dealer's left starts. On your turn:

1. Flip the top card of your draw pile **away from yourself** so opponents see it at the same time as you (or slightly before).
2. Place it face up on your personal discard stack so that **only the top card** is visible.

Play continues clockwise, building one face-up stack per player.

### When to Ring the Bell

Ring the bell as soon as the **top** cards of all face-up stacks show **exactly five fruits of the same type** in total (count across all visible tops).

Example (4 players): tops show 2 bananas + 3 bananas → exactly **5 bananas** → ring!

### Winning the Piles

The first player to ring correctly takes **all** face-up discard stacks, puts them face down under their draw pile, then flips a new card to start the next sequence.

### Sorry! (False Ring)

If you ring when there are **not** exactly five of one fruit, give **each other player** one card from your face-down draw pile as a penalty.

### Out of Cards

When your draw pile is empty you stop flipping, but you are **not** out yet: your face-up stack stays until someone wins a round and takes it. You may still ring the bell. Once your face-up stack is also taken and you have no cards left, you drop out.

## End of the Game

When only **two** players remain, continue until one of them correctly rings and takes the face-up stacks. That player (or the one with more cards) wins — count cards; most cards wins.

**Special (final duel):** If a player rings falsely ("Sorry!") while only two remain, the **opponent** takes all face-up stacks and the game ends.

Optional house rule: play until one player holds **all** the cards.

## Tips

- Flip away from yourself — reaction speed is the whole game.
- Count **across all tops**, not just your own card.
- Exactly five means **not** four and **not** six — overshooting is a false ring.
