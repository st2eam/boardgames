# Harmonies Rules

## Overview

**Harmonies** is a tile-laying strategy game by designer Johan Benvenuto, published by **Libellud** (2024). Players build vibrant 3D landscapes on their personal boards by placing colored terrain tokens, then populate them with animal cubes when the terrain matches the habitat patterns on their animal cards.

## Components

- **1 Central Board** — double-sided (solo side / 2–4 player side)
- **4 Personal Boards** — double-sided (Side A with rivers / Side B with islands)
- **120 Wooden Tokens** — 23 blue (water), 23 gray (mountain), 21 brown (wood), 19 green (treetop), 19 yellow (field), 15 red (building)
- **32 Animal Cards** — each showing a habitat pattern and cube spaces
- **10 Nature Spirit Cards** — optional advanced variant
- **75 Animal Cubes** + 4 Nature Spirit Cubes
- **1 Cloth Pouch** — for drawing tokens
- 4 Reminder Cards & Scoresheet Pad

## Setup

1. **Central Board** — Place in the middle with the 2–4 player side face-up (use solo side for 1 player).
2. **Tokens** — Place all 120 tokens in the pouch, mix well. Draw 3 tokens randomly onto each of the 5 spaces on the central board.
3. **Animal Cards** — Shuffle and place 5 face-up next to the central board. The rest form a face-down draw pile.
4. **Cubes** — Place all animal cubes within reach.
5. **Personal Boards** — Each player takes a personal board. All must use the **same side** (Side A recommended for beginners).
6. **Start Player** — The last person to have seen a magnificent landscape goes first. Point the arrow on the central board toward them.

## How to Play — Your Turn

On your turn, take actions in any order. One is mandatory; two are optional:

| Action | Type |
|---|---|
| **Take and Place Tokens** | MANDATORY |
| **Take an Animal Card** | Optional (max 1 per turn) |
| **Place Animal Cubes** | Optional (unlimited times) |

At turn's end: refill the empty central board space with 3 new tokens from the pouch, and replenish animal cards back to 5 face-up.

---

### 1. Take and Place Tokens (Mandatory)

Choose **one space** on the central board and take all 3 tokens. You **must place all three** on your personal board by the end of your turn. You may interleave placing tokens with optional actions.

#### Stacking Rules

Tokens can be stacked up to **3 high**:

| Token | Can be placed on | Can have placed on top |
|---|---|---|
| 🔵 **Blue (Water)** | Empty space only | Nothing |
| 🟡 **Yellow (Fields)** | Empty space only | Nothing |
| ⚫ **Gray (Mountains)** | Empty space or other gray | Only other gray |
| 🟤 **Brown (Wood/Trunk)** | Empty space or other brown | Brown or green |
| 🟢 **Green (Treetop)** | Only on 1 or 2 brown | Nothing |
| 🔴 **Red (Buildings)** | On gray, brown, or red (1 token below only) | Nothing (can't be 3rd in stack) |

> Once an animal cube is placed on a space, nothing can be stacked on top of it.

---

### 2. Take an Animal Card (Optional)

- Choose **1** of the 5 face-up cards and place it above your personal board.
- **Maximum 4 cards** at any time.
- Take the number of animal cubes shown on the card's right edge and place them on the card spaces (from bottom to top).

---

### 3. Place Animal Cubes (Optional)

When the token pattern on your board **exactly matches** the habitat pattern on an animal card:

- Take the **lowest available cube** from that card.
- Place it on a corresponding token in the pattern.
- Patterns can be matched in **any rotation or mirroring**.
- Tree/mountain **heights must match exactly**.
- **Red (building)** tokens in patterns can be any valid building type.
- A token can be part of multiple animal patterns, but holds only **one cube**.
- Once placed, cubes stay — even if later stacking breaks the original pattern.
- When **all cubes are removed** from an animal card, discard it — it no longer counts toward your 4-card limit.

---

## End Game Triggers

The game ends when either:

1. The pouch is **empty** when you try to refill the central board, **OR**
2. A player has **2 or fewer empty spaces** on their personal board at turn's end.

All players get an equal number of turns. If triggered mid-round, remaining players get one final turn.

## Scoring

### 🌳 Trees

| Formation | Points |
|---|---|
| 1 brown (no green) | 0 |
| 2 brown (no green) | 0 |
| 1 green alone (bush) | 1 |
| 1 brown + 1 green (tree) | 3 |
| 2 brown + 1 green (tall tree) | 7 |

### ⛰️ Mountains

*Must be adjacent to at least one other mountain to score.*

| Height | Points |
|---|---|
| 1 token | 1 |
| 2 tokens | 3 |
| 3 tokens | 7 |
| Isolated (no adjacent mountain) | 0 |

### 🌾 Fields

- Each contiguous group of **2+ adjacent yellow tokens** = **5 points**.
- Larger fields don't score more — many small separated fields is optimal.

### 🏠 Buildings

- A building (red stacked on gray/brown/red) = **5 points** — *only if* adjacent to at least **3 tokens of different colors**.
- Only the **top token** of each adjacent stack counts for color variety.

### 💧 Water — Side A (River)

Score your **longest continuous chain** of blue tokens using the **shortest path** between two ends:

| Chain Length | Points |
|---|---|
| 1 | 0 |
| 2 | 2 |
| 3 | 5 |
| 4 | 8 |
| 5 | 11 |
| 6 | 15 |
| Each beyond 6 | +4 |

> ⚠️ Loops are penalized — shortest path through a loop roughly equals half the loop tokens + 1.

### 🏝️ Water — Side B (Islands)

- Each land area **separated** from others by blue tokens = **5 points**.
- You always have at least 1 island.

### 🐾 Animal Cards

For each animal card, score the **number in the highest uncovered cube space** (topmost empty circle). If no cubes were placed, the card scores **zero**.

### Tiebreaker

Most animal cubes placed wins. If still tied, shared victory.

## Nature Spirit Cards (Optional Variant)

Recommended after a few games:

- **Setup:** Each player draws 2 Spirit cards, keeps 1, returns the other to the box. Place it above your board (counts toward your 4-card limit). Place a Nature Spirit cube on it.
- **Gameplay:** Works like an animal card with a special pattern. Once you place the cube, the card moves aside like a completed animal card.
- **Scoring:** Two types:
    1. **Landscape count** — Score for each time you create a specific layout.
    2. **Group scoring** — Score for each group of connected tokens matching the card.

Spirit card points are **added** to all other scoring.

## Solo Mode

| Rule | Change |
|---|---|
| **Central Board** | Use the solo side (3 spaces instead of 5) |
| **Animal Cards** | Only 3 face-up |
| **Token Cleanup** | At each turn's start, remove all 6 leftover tokens from the central board (they go to the box, not the pouch), then refill all 3 spaces |
| **Card Refresh** | If you didn't take an animal card, you may discard 1 face-up card and replace it from the draw pile |

### Solo Scoring — Earn Suns (🌞):

| Score | Suns |
|---|---|
| 40+ | 1 🌞 |
| 70+ | 2 🌞 |
| 90+ | 3 🌞 |
| 110+ | 4 🌞 |
| 130+ | 5 🌞 |
| 140+ | 6 🌞 |
| 150+ | 7 🌞 |
| 160+ | 8 🌞 |

- Using Side A board: **+1 sun**
- No Nature Spirit card used: **+2 suns**
- Group-type Nature Spirit used: **+1 sun**

## Strategy Tips

- **Plan ahead** — place tokens in anticipation of animal cards in the display, but beware others may take them first.
- **Small fields > large fields** — break up yellow tokens for more 5-point field groups.
- **Balance** — animal cards and terrain scoring can conflict; find the right trade-offs.
- **Keep mountains adjacent** — isolated mountains score zero even if tall.
- **Buildings need color variety** — place them where diverse token colors surround them.
- **Water loops are bad** on Side A — aim for long, winding, non-looping rivers.
