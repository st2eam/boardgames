# Just Wild (King of the Wilderness) Rules

## Overview

Just Wild is a number puzzle and area control card game published by Helvetiq, designed by Marco Franchin and Leo Colovini. Players choose an endangered animal species — lynx, wolf, ibex, or bear — and strategically place numbered cards to expand their territory while covering opponents' animals. The player with the most visible half cards of their species at the end wins.

## Components

- 80 cards numbered 1–10 (20 per species: lynx, wolf, ibex, bear)
- 24 animal tokens (6 per species; each player uses 5)

Each card is double-sided: the front shows the card value and the animal, while the back shows two numbers whose sum equals the front value.

## Setup

1. Each player chooses an animal species and takes that species' 20-card deck and **5 tokens**. Return unused cards and tokens to the box.
2. Each player shuffles their deck, draws the **top card**, and places it **face up** in the center of the table. All cards must be adjacent and form a rectangle.
3. Draw **3 cards** as your starting hand. Place the rest of your deck face down as your **personal draw pile**.
4. The last player to have spotted a wild animal starts the game.

## How to Play

Players take turns clockwise. On your turn, you must place a card on the table, then **refill your hand to 3 cards** from your personal draw pile.

### Option A: Play a Card Face Up (Cover)

Place a card **face up** so that it covers **exactly half of two adjacent cards**. The value on your card must equal the **sum of the two covered halves**.

- You are **not allowed** to entirely cover a single card.

**When the sum exceeds 10:** You must play **2 cards** — first play one card face up covering the two halves, then play the top card from your draw pile **face down** anywhere adjacent to an existing card. The face-down card counts as value 10, which is added to your face-up card's value to reach the required sum. (If your draw pile is empty, you cannot perform this action.)

### Option B: Play a Card Face Down (Expand)

If you cannot or choose not to cover any cards, play one card from your hand **face down** adjacent to any existing card on the table. Face-down cards can be covered on future turns following normal rules.

### Special Cards (Baby Animals)

Each species has 10 special cards showing baby animals (numbered 1–10). When you play a baby animal card **face up**, you may place one of your **tokens** on one of the two half cards it covers. A token-protected half card **can never be covered** for the rest of the game.

- You only have **5 tokens** — use them wisely.
- Once placed, tokens cannot be moved or taken back.

## Card Back Information

The back of every card shows two numbers whose sum equals the front value. This means you can deduce the front value of face-down cards (yours or opponents') by reading the numbers on the back.

## End of Game & Scoring

Players do **not** end the game simultaneously. When you play your **last card**:

1. Count your score immediately: each **visible half card** of your species on the table = **1 point**.
2. Record your score — it is final even if your cards get covered later by other players.

After all players have played all their cards, compare scores. The player with the **highest score** wins.

**Tiebreaker:** The player with the most **unused tokens** wins. If still tied, the victory is shared.
