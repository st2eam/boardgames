# Love Letter Rules

## Theme

In *Love Letter*, 2–6 suitors compete to have their missives delivered to the kingdom’s princess, who seeks an ideal partner and confidant for when she assumes the throne.

The card in your hand represents the person who currently carries your letter. This can change during the round as you draw and play cards.

To win a round, you must either have the **highest-value card** at the end of the round or be the **only remaining player**.

> This page covers the **Full Game** edition (**Princess = 9**, **21 cards**), for **2–6 players**. A classic 2–4 player variant is noted at the end.

## Components

- **21 character cards** (see Full Game table below)
- **6 reference cards**
- **13 favor tokens** (♥)
- **1 cloth bag**

## Setup

1. Give each player a **reference card** (return extras to the bag).
2. Shuffle the **21 character cards** to form the deck. Place it face down within easy reach, along with the favor tokens.
3. Take the **top card** of the deck and set it aside **face down** without looking.  
   **2 players only:** also take the next **3 cards** and set them aside **face up**.
4. Deal each player **one card** as their starting hand.
5. The player who most recently **hand-wrote a letter** takes the first turn.

## Game Play

Love Letter is played over several rounds. Take turns in clockwise order.

### Player Turn

On your turn:

1. **Draw** one card from the deck (you now hold two).
2. **Choose and play** one of your two cards, resolving its effect immediately.
3. The played card stays in your play area **face up**; keep the other card in hand.

Played and discarded cards must **always remain visible** so everyone can track what remains in the deck.

### Out of the Round

Some effects knock you out of the current round. When this happens:

- Discard your remaining hand card **face up** (without resolving its effect — except the Princess, which still knocks you out if discarded for any reason).
- Until the next round, you cannot be chosen for card effects, and your turns are skipped.

## Card Effects — Full Game (2–6 Players)

| Value | № | Name | Effect description |
|:-----:|:-:|------|--------------------|
| 9 | 1 | **Princess** | If you play or discard this card, you are out of the round. |
| 8 | 1 | **Countess** | If the King or Prince is in your hand, you must play this card. |
| 7 | 1 | **King** | Choose and trade hands with another player. |
| 6 | 2 | **Chancellor** | Draw 2 cards. Keep 1 card and put your other 2 on the bottom of the deck in any order. |
| 5 | 2 | **Prince** | Choose any player (including yourself). That player discards their hand and redraws. |
| 4 | 2 | **Handmaid** | Until the start of your next turn, other players cannot choose you for their card effects. |
| 3 | 2 | **Baron** | Choose and secretly compare hands with another player. Whoever has the lower value is out of the round. |
| 2 | 2 | **Priest** | Choose and look at another player's hand. |
| 1 | 6 | **Guard** | Choose another player and name a non-Guard card. If that player has that card, they are out of the round. |
| 0 | 2 | **Spy** | At the end of the round, if you are the only player still in the round who played or discarded a Spy, gain 1 favor token. |

### Card Notes

- **Princess (9):** Out if played or discarded for any reason (including when forced by the Prince).
- **Countess (8):** Has no effect when played or discarded. You **must** play her if your other card is King or Prince; you may also play her voluntarily. Do not reveal your other card. Her restriction **does not apply** when drawing for other effects (Chancellor).
- **King (7):** Choose another player and trade hands. If all other players still in the round are protected by a Handmaid, this card does nothing.
- **Chancellor (6):** Draw 2 so you temporarily hold 3; keep 1 and secretly place the other 2 face down on the bottom of the deck in any order. If only **one** card remains in the deck, draw it and return one card. If the deck is **empty**, this card has no effect.
- **Prince (5):** The chosen player discards face up **without resolving** that card’s play effect, then draws a new hand. If the deck is empty, they draw the face-down set-aside card. If forced to discard the Princess, they are immediately out and **do not** draw a new hand. If all other players are Handmaid-protected, you must choose yourself.
- **Handmaid (4):** Protection lasts until the **start** of your next turn. If all other players still in the round are protected when you play a card that requires choosing **another** player (Guard, Priest, Baron, King), that card has no effect. If the card requires choosing **any** player (Prince), you must choose yourself.
- **Baron (3):** On a tie, neither player is out. If all other players still in the round are Handmaid-protected, this card does nothing.
- **Priest (2):** Look at the hand privately — do **not** reveal it to anyone else.
- **Guard (1):** Name a character **other than Guard**. The chosen player announces whether they have that card. If all other players still in the round are Handmaid-protected, this card does nothing.
- **Spy (0):** No effect when played or discarded. Favor is checked at round end among players **still in** the round. Spy favor **does not count as winning the round** — the round winner still gains their token (even if it is you). Two Spies still award at most 1 favor.

## End of a Round

A round ends in one of two ways:

### Deck Runs Out

After any turn, if the deck is empty, all players still in the round reveal and compare their hands.

- Highest-value card wins the round and gains **1 favor token**.
- **On a tie, all tied players win the round and each gains 1 favor token.**

### One Player Left

If you are the only player still in the round, the round ends immediately and you gain **1 favor token**.

> **Spy favor:** Resolved separately after the round winner(s) are determined. It does not replace or cancel a round win.

### Starting the Next Round

Repeat setup steps 2–4 (shuffle, set cards aside, deal one card each). The player who won the previous round takes the first turn. If there was a tie, randomly decide among the tied players who goes first.

## Winning the Game

The game ends when a player has enough favor tokens (based on player count). **Multiple players can simultaneously win.**

| № Players | Favor tokens to win |
|:---------:|:-------------------:|
| 2 | 6 |
| 3 | 5 |
| 4 | 4 |
| 5 | 3 |
| 6 | 3 |

## Classic Version (Optional)

For the classic game (**2–4 players** only), before setup return these cards to the bag:

- **1 Guard**
- **2 Chancellors**
- **2 Spies**

All other rules are unchanged.
