# Modern Art Rules

## Overview

Modern Art is an auction game designed by Reiner Knizia. Players take on the role of art dealers buying and selling paintings from five different artists over four rounds. Each round, paintings are auctioned off using different auction types. At the end of each round, paintings are sold to the bank at market value. The player with the most money at the end wins.

## Components

- 70 painting cards (5 artists)
- Money tokens (in various denominations)
- 1 market value board
- 12 artist value tiles
- 5 player screens

## Artists & Card Distribution

| Artist | Cards |
|--------|-------|
| Manuel Carvalho (Lite Metal) | 12 |
| Sigrid Thaler (Yoko) | 13 |
| Daniel Melim (Christin P.) | 14 |
| Ramon Martins (Karl Gitter) | 15 |
| Rafael Silveira (Krypto) | 16 |

*(Artist names vary by edition; card counts remain the same.)*

## Setup

1. Each player receives **100** in starting money (hidden behind their screen).
2. Place the market value board in the center.
3. Shuffle all 70 painting cards and deal starting hands based on player count:
   - **3 players**: 10 cards each
   - **4 players**: 9 cards each
   - **5 players**: 8 cards each
4. Place remaining cards face down as the draw pile.
5. The youngest player goes first as the Auctioneer.

## How to Play

On your turn, play one painting card from your hand and conduct the auction indicated on the card.

## Auction Types

### Open Auction
Starting from the player to the left of the Auctioneer, players bid freely. Each bid must exceed the previous. The highest bidder pays the Auctioneer and takes the painting. If no one bids, the Auctioneer gets it for free.

### Once Around
Starting left of the Auctioneer, each player gets exactly one chance to bid or pass. The Auctioneer bids last. Highest bid wins.

### Sealed Auction
All players (including the Auctioneer) secretly hold money in their fist. Reveal simultaneously. Highest bidder wins. Ties go to the player closest to the Auctioneer's left.

### Fixed Price
The Auctioneer sets a price. Starting left, each player may buy at that price or pass. If no one buys, the Auctioneer **must** buy it at that price from the bank.

### Double Auction
The Auctioneer plays the card and may pair it with a second painting **from the same artist** in their hand. If they cannot or choose not to, the next player clockwise may contribute a matching card. The pair is auctioned as one lot using Open Auction rules. The player who contributed the second card splits the payment with the Auctioneer.

## Rounds & Scoring

A round ends immediately when the **5th painting** of any single artist is played. That 5th card is **not** auctioned — it is discarded.

### Market Value

After each round, rank the top 3 artists by number of paintings sold (auctioned) that round:
- **1st place**: 30,000 per painting
- **2nd place**: 20,000 per painting
- **3rd place**: 10,000 per painting
- **4th–5th place**: 0 (worthless)

Place the corresponding value tile (30/20/10) on the market value board in that artist's column for the current round. Ties are broken by the order of artists on the board.

### Cumulative Value

An artist's market value is the **sum of all value tiles** in their column across all rounds played so far. So an artist who placed 1st in round 1 (30) and 2nd in round 2 (20) would be worth 50 per painting in round 2.

### Selling to the Bank

After determining values, each player sells all paintings they purchased this round to the bank at their current market value. Unsold paintings from your hand are worthless.

### New Round

Deal additional cards from the draw pile:
- **Rounds 2 & 3** — 3 players: 6 cards; 4 players: 4 cards; 5 players: 3 cards
- **Round 4** — No new cards dealt

## End Game

After 4 rounds, the player with the **most total money** (including all earnings from painting sales) wins.
