# Odin Rules

## Overview

**Odin** is a fast ladder-climbing / shedding card game from Helvetiq (designers: **Gary Kim, Hope S. Hwang & Yohan Goh**). Play higher-value sets than the cards in the middle, pick up one card you just beat, and empty your hand. Fewest points after several hands wins.

Chinese edition title: **维京怒火**.

**Goal:** Discard all cards from your hand as often as you can. When someone reaches the point threshold (usually **15**), the player with the **fewest** points wins.

## Components

- **54 cards** numbered **1–9** in **6 colours** (one of each number per colour)

## Setup

1. Shuffle all cards.
2. Deal **9 cards** face down to each player (your hand — look at them, keep them secret).
3. Set remaining cards aside for later hands.
4. Choose a first player at random. Turns proceed clockwise.

**2-player variant:** Remove **2 colours** from the deck for a quicker, tighter game.

## How to Play

The game is played over several **hands**. Each hand is divided into **rounds**.

### Starting a Round

The lead player plays **1 card** face up in the middle of the table.

### On Your Turn

Choose one:

1. **Play** one or more cards, or
2. **Pass**

#### Playing Cards

All of the following must be true:

- Play the **same number of cards** as currently in the middle, **or exactly one more**.
  - Example: middle has 2 cards → you may play 2 or 3 (not 1, not 4).
- If you play multiple cards, they must share the **same number** or the **same colour**.
- The **value** of your play must be **strictly greater** than the value in the middle.

**Multi-card value:** Treat each card's number as a digit and form the **highest** possible number.

| Cards played | Value |
|--------------|------:|
| 2 + 8 (same colour) | **82** |
| 2 + 4 + 9 (same colour) | **942** |
| Single 7 | **7** |

#### After You Play

You must pick up **1 card** from the middle (not one you just played):

- If only 1 card was there before your play, take that card into your hand.
- If a set was there, choose 1 of those beaten cards for your hand and **discard the rest**.

#### Pass

Play no cards. The next player takes their turn. Passing only applies to this turn — you may play again later.

If **all but one** player pass consecutively, discard any cards left in the middle. Keep hands as-is. The last player who played starts a new round (leads with 1 card).

## End of a Hand

A hand ends in either way:

1. **Empty hand:** You play card(s) and have **no cards left**. Do **not** pick up a card from the middle. The hand ends immediately.
2. **Lead with a pure hand:** When you start a new round and **all** cards remaining in your hand share the same number **or** the same colour, you may play them **all at once**. The hand ends immediately. If you cannot, just lead with 1 card as usual.

### Scoring

Each player scores **1 point per card left in hand**. Track cumulative points.

### Next Hand

Collect and shuffle all cards. Deal 9 to each player. The player to the **left** of the previous starting player leads the new hand.

## End of the Game

Recommended for a first game: play until a player reaches **15** or more points. Then the player with the **fewest** points wins (ties share victory).

| Length | Play to |
|--------|---------|
| Short | 10 |
| Standard | 15 |
| Long | 20 |
| Ultra-short | 1 hand only |

## Tips

- Climbing by **one extra card** can create huge multi-digit values.
- When you beat a set, choose carefully which card to pick up — it reshapes your next plays.
- Passing can force others to climb higher and leave you a cleaner lead later.
