# Sea Salt & Paper Rules

## Overview

Sea Salt & Paper is a set-collection and push-your-luck card game for 2-4 players. Players draw cards, play Duo pairs for effects, collect sets for points, and choose the perfect moment to end each round. The game is played over multiple rounds until one player reaches the target score.

## Components

- 58 playing cards (origami-style sea creatures)
- 4 player aid cards

## Setup

1. Shuffle all 58 cards into a face-down draw pile.
2. Reveal the top 2 cards face-up beside the deck to form **two separate discard piles**.
3. Choose a starting player randomly.
4. Set the **target score** based on player count:
   - 2 players: **40 points**
   - 3 players: **35 points**
   - 4 players: **30 points**

## Turn Structure

On your turn, perform these steps in order:

### Step 1: Take a Card (Mandatory)

Choose ONE:
- **Draw from deck:** Draw the top 2 cards, keep 1, place the other face-up on either discard pile. (If one pile is empty, you must place it there.)
- **Take from discard:** Take the top card from one of the two discard piles.

**Important:** You may not browse through discard piles (only the top card is available).

### Step 2: Play Duo Cards (Optional)

You may play one or more **Duo pairs** from your hand face-up in front of you to trigger their effects (see Duo Cards below). You may play multiple pairs in the same turn.

### Step 3: End the Round (Optional)

If your total points (cards in hand + cards played in front of you) are **7 or more**, you may end the round by declaring one of two options:

#### Option A: "STOP"
The round ends **immediately**. All players reveal their hands and score their full card points.

#### Option B: "LAST CHANCE"
Each other player gets **one final turn**, then everyone reveals.
- If the caller has the **highest or tied-highest score:** The caller scores their card points **+ color bonus**. All other players score **only** their color bonus.
- If the caller does **NOT** have the highest score: The caller scores **only** their color bonus. All other players score their **full card points**.

**Color Bonus:** 1 point for each card of the color you have the most of (among cards in hand and in front of you).

## Card Types & Scoring

All cards score whether they are in your hand or played in front of you.

### Duo Cards

Duos score **1 point per matching pair** regardless of whether they are played or kept in hand. Playing them face-up triggers their special effect.

| Duo | Qty | Effect (when played) |
|-----|:---:|------|
| **Crab** | 9 | Choose one discard pile, browse it freely (without shuffling), and take any 1 card. Other players don't see your choice. |
| **Boat** | 8 | Take another turn immediately after this one. |
| **Fish** | 7 | Draw the top card from the deck into your hand. |
| **Swimmer + Shark** | 5+5 | Steal 1 random card from another player's hand. |

### Collector Cards

Score based on how many of each type you collect. Points increase with quantity:

| Type | Qty in deck | Points by count (1/2/3/4/5/6) |
|------|:-----------:|------|
| **Shell** | 6 | 0 / 2 / 4 / 6 / 8 / 10 |
| **Octopus** | 5 | 0 / 3 / 6 / 9 / 12 |
| **Penguin** | 3 | 1 / 3 / 5 |
| **Sailor** | 2 | 0 / 5 |

### Multiplier Cards (1 of each)

Each multiplier scores bonus points based on how many matching cards you have:

| Multiplier | Scores |
|-----------|--------|
| **Lighthouse** | 1 point per Boat card |
| **Shoal of Fish** | 1 point per Fish card |
| **Penguin Colony** | 2 points per Penguin card |
| **The Captain** | 3 points per Sailor card |

### Mermaid Cards (×4)

Each Mermaid scores **1 point per card of your most abundant color** (counting cards in hand + in front of you).

- With multiple Mermaids, each must count a **different color** (1st Mermaid = most common color, 2nd = second most common, etc.)
- Mermaid cards themselves are **white** and can be counted as a color group.
- **Instant win:** If any player has all 4 Mermaids (in hand or played), they **immediately win the game**.

---

## End of Round

1. Add each player's round score to their cumulative total.
2. If any player has reached or exceeded the target score, the game ends — highest total wins. Ties are broken by the highest score in the final round.
3. Otherwise, shuffle all cards, reform the deck and discard piles, and start a new round. The player to the left of the one who ended the previous round goes first.

**Deck runs out:** If the draw pile is exhausted during a player's turn, the round ends immediately with **no scoring** for anyone.
