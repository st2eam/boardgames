# Splendor: Pokémon Rules

## Overview

Splendor: Pokémon is a standalone variant of Splendor themed around the Pokémon universe. Players are Pokémon trainers collecting Pokéballs to catch Pokémon, build their team, and attract Legendary Pokémon. The core mechanics remain the same as original Splendor, with key additions: an **evolution mechanic**, **Rare & Legendary Pokémon**, and a higher victory threshold of **18 points**. Does not require the original Splendor.

## What's Different from Original Splendor

| | Original Splendor | Pokémon Edition |
|---|---|---|
| **Theme** | Gem merchants | Pokémon trainers |
| **Resources** | Gem tokens | Pokéball tokens (5 colors) |
| **Wild token** | Gold | Master Ball |
| **Cards** | Development cards | Pokémon cards (3 levels) |
| **Nobles** | Noble tiles | Rare & Legendary Pokémon |
| **Victory points** | 15 to win | 18 to win |
| **Evolution** | None | New mechanic (see below) |

## Setup

Same as original Splendor: 3 levels of Pokémon cards (4 face-up each), Pokéball tokens in the center, and Rare/Legendary Pokémon revealed. Each player receives a **Trainer tile** (used as a removed-cards zone for evolution).

## Turn Actions

Same four actions as original Splendor:

1. **Collect 3 different Pokéballs** (3 different colors)
2. **Collect 2 same-color Pokéballs** (requires ≥4 of that color available)
3. **Reserve a Pokémon** + take 1 Master Ball (wild)
4. **Catch a Pokémon** by paying its Pokéball cost

Token limit remains **10 per player**.

## New Mechanic: Evolution

At the **end of your turn** (after checking the token limit), you may evolve **one** of your caught Pokémon:

### Evolution Requirements
1. The Pokémon's evolved form must be available — either face-up in the display **or** in your reserved cards.
2. You must have enough **Pokéball bonuses** (from caught Pokémon) matching the evolution cost shown on the card. **Tokens cannot be used for evolution — only permanent bonuses count.**

### How Evolution Works
1. Take the evolved Pokémon card and place it in front of you (it provides its own points and bonuses).
2. Place the pre-evolution Pokémon card **under your Trainer tile** — it is removed from the game. It no longer provides points or bonuses.
3. If the evolved form came from the display, refill from the corresponding deck.

### Evolution Rules
- You may evolve at most **1 Pokémon per turn**
- Evolution is **optional** — you can skip it even if eligible
- Level 1 Pokémon evolve to Level 2; Level 2 evolve to Level 3
- Bonuses used for evolution are **not consumed** (they remain permanent)

## Rare & Legendary Pokémon

These replace Noble tiles from the original game.

| Type | Display | Bonuses | Master Ball Required | Points |
|------|---------|---------|:-------------------:|--------|
| **Rare** | 1 face-up at a time | 2 permanent bonuses | No | Lower |
| **Legendary** | 1 face-up at a time | 2 permanent bonuses | Yes (1 Master Ball) | Higher |

### Key Rules
- Rare and Legendary Pokémon **cannot be reserved**
- They are caught like regular Pokémon (pay the cost), but Legendary ones additionally require spending 1 Master Ball
- Each provides **2 permanent Pokéball bonuses** instead of the usual 1
- When caught, immediately reveal the next one from the corresponding deck

## Game End

When any player reaches **18 or more points**, complete the current round.

**Winner:** Most points.

**Tiebreakers (in order):**
1. More Pokémon cards (including those under Trainer tile)
2. More evolutions performed

## Strategy Tips

- Evolution is very powerful — catching Level 1 Pokémon with 0 points can set up high-value evolutions later
- Master Balls are crucial for Legendary Pokémon — reserve cards strategically to collect them
- Rare & Legendary Pokémon provide 2 bonuses each, making them efficient for building your engine
