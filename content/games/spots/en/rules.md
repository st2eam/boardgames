# Spots Rules

## Overview

Spots is a push-your-luck dice game for 1–4 players. You roll dice and place them on matching spaces on your dog cards. Fill all the spots on your dogs to score them, but watch out — if your buried dice total more than 7, you bust and lose everything! The first player to score 6 dog cards wins.

## Components

- Dog cards (each showing 2–4 numbered dice spaces)
- 6 trick tiles (action tiles)
- Additional trick tiles (for game variety)
- Custom dice
- Treats (reroll tokens)
- Yard boards (for burying dice)

## Setup

1. Take out these 6 trick tiles and place them face-up in the center: **Howl, Walk, Run, Roll Over, Fetch, Beg**.
2. Shuffle the dog card deck and deal **2 dog cards** to each player, gray side up. Place the rest as a draw pile.
3. Place all dice and treats in shared piles within reach.
4. Each player takes **1 yard board**, **1 treat**, and **1 die**. Roll the die and bury it in your yard.
5. Whoever has the highest buried die goes first (reroll ties).

## How to Play

Players take turns clockwise. On your turn, choose one of two actions:

### Action 1: Do a Trick

1. Choose any face-up trick tile.
2. Flip it face-down and follow all steps in order.

Most tricks involve rolling dice and placing them on your dogs.

#### Rolling and Placing Dice

- Take dice from the common pile (unless the trick says otherwise).
- After rolling, place dice on **matching spaces** on any of your dogs (e.g., a die showing 4 goes on a space marked 4).
- Any dice you can't or don't want to place must be **buried in your yard**.

#### Spending Treats (Rerolling)

Immediately after rolling (before placing/burying), you may spend **1 treat** to reroll:

- You must reroll **ALL** dice from that roll — you can't keep some.
- No limit on treats spent per turn — keep rerolling as long as you have treats.
- You can only reroll the most recent roll.

#### Busting

If the total spots on your **buried dice exceed 7**, you bust!

When you bust:
- Your turn ends immediately (even mid-trick).
- **Discard all dice** on your dogs.
- **Discard all buried dice.**

### Action 2: Score Your Dogs

Instead of doing a trick, you can secure your progress by scoring.

1. Discard all dice on your **completed** dog cards (all spaces filled).
2. Flip those dogs face-down (scored).
3. Draw **1 new dog** for each dog scored, until you have up to 6 total cards (scored + unscored).

You must have at least one completed dog to score. When scoring, you must score **all** completed dogs.

### Fast and Risky Scoring

If you fill **all** your face-up dogs during a trick, score them **instantly** — no need to spend a turn!

- You must first place or bury all dice from your most recent roll. If that roll busts you, no scoring.
- After fast scoring, continue the rest of the trick (draw new dogs and keep going if steps remain).

## Resetting Trick Tiles

When only **1 face-up trick tile** remains at the start of your turn:

1. Place **1 treat** from the supply onto the last face-up tile.
2. Flip **all** trick tiles face-up.

The treat goes to whoever next chooses that tile.

## Other Rules

- **Dog card limit:** You can never have more than 6 dog cards (scored + unscored). Stop drawing once you reach 6.
- **Running out of dice/treats:** Take as many as available. If zero dice exist at the start of your turn, all players bust and lose their dice.
- **Trick steps:** Steps are mandatory unless they say "may." If you can't perform a step, skip it. Some steps can be repeated until you decide to stop (or bust).
- **Paw prints:** Some trick tiles are stronger based on the number of paw print icons on your dog cards.

## Winning the Game

The first player to have **6 scored dog cards** wins immediately!

## Solo Mode

Play against an AI opponent named **d0g**. d0g starts with 1 treat, no yard, and no dogs. You go first.

On d0g's turn, roll a die. Imagine trick tiles labeled 1–6:
- If the matching tile is face-up → d0g flips it down and takes any treats on it.
- If the matching tile is face-down → d0g scores the top dog card from the deck.

When only 1 tile is face-up for d0g, reset tiles first. If d0g has 2+ treats, it returns 2 and scores the top dog card.

You win by scoring 6 dogs. You lose if d0g scores 6 first. Adjust difficulty by changing d0g's starting treats.

## Variant: Adding Variety

After playing with the starter trick tiles, swap in others from the box:

- Always include **Howl** and **Roll Over**.
- Add 1 tile from each of the 4 color categories.

Recommended sets include: Adoption Event, Dog Park, Agility Course, Hunting Grounds, Kennel Club, and Puppy Pen.
