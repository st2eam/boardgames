# TACTA Rules

## Overview

TACTA is a sneakily strategic card placement game published by The Op (USAopoly). Players flip, twist, and turn their cards to align shapes and cover their opponents' dots while protecting their own. When all cards are played, the player with the most visible dots of their color wins.

## Components

- 108 playing cards (6 colored decks of 18 cards each)
- 1 Starting card (marked with an X)

## Setup

1. Choose a table or flat play area with ample space. The table edge marks the boundary — cards cannot go beyond it.
2. Place the **Starting card** in the center of the play area.
3. Sort cards by color. Each player takes one color deck (18 cards) and shuffles it.
4. Hold your deck as a stack in your hand. **Never fan or look through your cards.** You may only access the two outermost cards (top and bottom) at any time.
5. The player with the lowest value on either of their outermost cards goes first. Play proceeds clockwise.

## How to Play

On your turn, choose one of your two accessible cards (top or bottom of your deck) and play it to the table.

### Placing a Card

- You may **flip and/or rotate** your card freely before placing.
- Your card must **connect to exactly one card** already on the table by covering one of its shapes.
- When shapes match and align correctly, the **outline of the underlying card appears continuous** — this confirms a legal placement.
- Hollow shapes and dot-filled shapes are interchangeable for matching purposes.
- A card may **only connect to one** existing card (not bridge two cards).

### When You Cannot Play

If neither of your accessible cards can legally cover any shape on the table, play one card **face-up anywhere** on the table that does not touch any existing card.

### Illegal Plays

If an illegal card placement is discovered, remove the card — unless another card has already been placed on top of it, in which case it stays.

## Scoring

When all players have played all their cards, count the **visible dots of your color** on the table. That is your score.

The player with the **most visible dots** wins. Ties are shared victories.

## Variants

### Speed Mode (No Turns)
All players play simultaneously. Countdown 3-2-1, Go! Play as fast as you can following all placement rules. You cannot play on a card of your own color. The game ends when any player plays their last card.

### Team Mode
Play in teams with teammates sitting across from each other. Each teammate uses a 9-card half-deck.

### Three-Round Mode
Separate cards by suit symbol. Play 3 rounds using a different suit each round. Total dots across all rounds determines the winner.
