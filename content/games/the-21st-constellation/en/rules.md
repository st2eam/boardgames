# The 21st Constellation Rules

## Overview

Stars hold magic, Starweaver. Summon the constellations and channel their power!

The 21st Constellation is a card game combining the classic "21" (Blackjack) push-your-luck mechanic with magical spell cards. Players draw "Star Cards" to accumulate points as close to 21 as possible without exceeding it, while using "Spell Cards" to change the tide of battle.

### Core Concepts

- **Star Cards**: Represent star points placed on your field. Draw to get closer to 21 without going over.
- **Spell Cards**: Represent magical effects that can alter your or opponents' star cards.
- **Chanting (Pass)**: Taking no action in a turn. When ALL players pass in a single round, the game ends and scoring begins.

---

## Player Counts

### 2 Players: Duel · 1v1

Use a single suit of Star Cards (one each of 1–K). Remove 2 Spell Cards K and 2 Spell Cards Q.

### 3 Players: Dark Sorcerer · 1v2

Use the full Star Card deck (1–K, two of each). After setup, one player may volunteer to become the "Dark Sorcerer," drawing 2 extra Spell Cards. The Dark Sorcerer's Star Resonance rewards are doubled.

### 4 Players: Campaign · 2v2

Use the full Star Card deck (1–K, two of each). Players sitting across from each other are teammates.

---

## Setup

1. **Separate decks**: Sort cards by their backs into a Star Card pile and a Spell Card pile.
2. **Draw starting hands**:
   - Each player draws **4 Spell Cards** as their starting hand.
   - Each player draws **2 Star Cards**, placed left to right:
     - First card is your **hole card** (face-down, only you may look).
     - Second card (and all future Star Cards) are placed **face-up**.
3. **Determine first player**: The player with the lowest face-up Star Card total goes first.

> Note: Club/Spade-suited Star Card K/Q do not count when comparing opening totals.

---

## Turn Structure

On your turn, choose ONE of the following:

### Option 1: Play Spell Cards

- Play one or more Spell Cards (no limit per turn).
- After playing spells, you may still choose to draw a Star Card.

### Option 2: Draw a Star Card

- Draw one Star Card and place it face-up. Your turn ends immediately.
- You may also choose not to draw, ending your turn.

### Pass (Chanting)

- Do nothing — take no action.
- **When ALL players pass in a full round, proceed to Final Scoring.**

---

## Star Resonance

When your face-up Star Cards form specific patterns, "Star Resonance" triggers and you draw bonus Spell Cards:

| Pattern | Condition | Reward |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| Pair | Two Star Cards with the same value | Draw 1 Spell Card |
| Sequence | Consecutively increasing values | Draw 1 Spell Card |
| Suited Sequence | Sequence of the same suit (multiplayer only) | Draw 1 extra Spell Card |

### Additional Rules

- Maximum of **5 card slots** per player.
- Changing a Star Card's value via Spell Cards can also trigger Star Resonance.
- Mark triggered cards by pushing them up slightly.
- **The same card slot cannot trigger Star Resonance more than once.**

---

## Final Scoring (Power of the Stars)

All players reveal their hole cards. Sum all Star Card values on each player's field.

### Scoring Table

| Rank | Points |
|------|--------|
| 1st place | 5 points |
| 2nd place | 4 points |
| 3rd place | 1 point |
| 4th place | 1 point |
| Bust (over 21) | -5 points |

- Exactly hitting the target (21) grants a **+1 bonus point**.
- Rankings are determined by proximity to 21.
- In 2v2 mode, the team with the higher combined score wins.

---

## Strategy Tips

- Your hole card is hidden information — use it to bluff.
- Spell Cards are the key to turning the tide; don't waste them.
- Watch opponents' face-up cards to estimate their totals.
- Star Resonance is your main source of extra Spell Cards — try to trigger it.
- Passing is also a strategy — when you're happy with your total, force opponents to act first.
