# Tic Tac Trek Rules

## Overview

Designed by **Brett J. Gilbert** and **Trevor Benjamin**, published by **Alley Cat Games**, with art by **Rory Muldoon**. Explore the wilderness in this 2-player twist on tic-tac-toe: draw terrain tiles, place your **X** or **O**, complete trails of three, and camp on the edge of the unexplored wilds. The more empty space around your campfires, the higher you score.

## Components

- **26 wooden terrain tiles** (double-sided: **X** on one side, **O** on the other)
  - Mountain ×6, Desert ×6, Grassland ×6, Forest ×6, Lake ×2
- **Campfire tokens** for each player
  - **O** player: 6 round bases + fire pieces
  - **X** player: 6 cross bases + fire pieces
- **1 cloth bag**
- **Rule sheet** (in tin)

## Setup

1. Place all **26 tiles** into the cloth bag and mix.
2. Draw **2 tiles** at random. Place them **orthogonally adjacent**, one showing **X** and the other showing **O** — this starts the shared play area.
3. Each player takes one symbol (**X** or **O**) and their **6 campfire tokens**.
4. Choose a starting player (or randomly). Play proceeds with alternating turns.

## How to Play

On your turn:

1. **Draw** 1 tile from the bag.
2. Orient it so **your symbol** (**X** or **O**) faces up.
3. **Place** the tile into the play area following the placement rules below.
4. If your placement completes one or more **treks** (three of your symbols in a row), place a campfire (see Campfires).

### Placement Rules

- Place the tile **orthogonally adjacent** to at least one existing tile.
- If possible, place it so that it **touches a matching terrain** type (or a **Lake**, which is wild and matches any terrain).
- **Lake tiles** may be placed adjacent to any terrain.
- If there is **no legal matching placement**, you may place the tile adjacent to any tile in the play area.

### Completing a Trek

A **trek** is three of **your symbols** in a straight line — horizontally, vertically, or diagonally. Terrain types do **not** need to match; only the symbols matter.

When the tile you just placed completes a trek:

- Place **one** of your campfire tokens on the tile you just placed.
- That tile (with a campfire) can **no longer** be part of any future trek.
- If you complete **two** treks with a single placement, you still place only **one** campfire.
- If all **6** of your campfires are already on the board, **move** one of them onto the newly placed tile instead.

## End of the Game & Scoring

The game ends when the **last tile** from the bag has been placed.

Score each of your campfires: for every **empty space** in the 8 surrounding positions (orthogonal and diagonal), score **1 point**. Spaces occupied by tiles do not score. A single campfire can score up to **8** points.

The player with the **highest total** wins.

### Ties

1. The player who placed the **most campfires** wins.
2. If still tied, the player who took the **final turn** loses.

## Notes

- More campfires are usually good, but a campfire surrounded by empty wilderness scores more than one buried in a dense grid — camping on the edge is the goal.
- Blocking your opponent’s lines while keeping your own campfires open is the core tension of the game.
