# TRIO Rules

## Overview

TRIO (originally published as *nana* in Japan) is a set-collection card game where players try to reveal three cards of the same number. Cards are hidden in the center of the table and in players' sorted hands — you can only ever access the lowest or highest card from any hand, making memory and deduction essential.

## Components

- 36 cards: numbers 1–12, three copies of each
- Each card shows connection icons in the corners indicating which other numbers are "connected" (sum or difference of 7)

## Setup

1. Choose a game mode: **Simple** or **Spicy** (recommended to start with Simple).
2. Shuffle all 36 cards.
3. Deal cards to each player based on player count:

| Players | Cards per Player | Cards in Center |
|---------|:----------------:|:---------------:|
| 3       | 9                | 9               |
| 4       | 7                | 8               |
| 5       | 6                | 6               |
| 6       | 5                | 6               |

4. Each player secretly arranges their hand from **lowest to highest** (this order must be maintained throughout the game).
5. Place the remaining cards face-down in the center of the table.

## How to Play

Players take turns clockwise. On your turn, you reveal numbered cards **one at a time**, trying to find three cards showing the same number.

### Revealing Cards

For each card you reveal, choose one of two options:

- **From the center:** Flip any face-down card in the middle of the table.
- **From a player's hand (including your own):** Ask any player to reveal their current **lowest** or **highest** card. The revealed card is placed face-up on the table.

**Important rules:**

- You may never reveal a card from the middle of anyone's hand — only the lowest or highest.
- You may perform the same action multiple times (e.g., ask the same player for their highest card repeatedly).
- Even when revealing from your own hand, it must be your lowest or highest card.
- If a player has no cards left, they still take turns using center cards and other players' cards.

### Turn Resolution

Your turn ends in one of two ways:

1. **Success — You complete a trio:** If you reveal three cards of the same number, take all three cards and place them face-up in front of you. Your turn ends.
2. **Bust — A mismatch:** As soon as any revealed card shows a different number from the previous one, your turn immediately ends. All revealed cards are returned to their original locations (face-down in the center or back into their owner's hand in the correct sorted position).

## Winning the Game

### Simple Mode

You win by achieving **either** of these:

- Collect **3 trios** (three sets of three-of-a-kind)
- Collect the **trio of 7s** (instant win, trumps everything)

### Spicy Mode

You win by achieving **either** of these:

- Collect **2 connected trios** — two trios whose numbers have a sum or difference of 7
- Collect the **trio of 7s** (instant win, trumps everything)

### Connected Trios Reference

Two trios are "connected" if their numbers add up to or have a difference of 7:

| Trio | Connected to |
|:----:|:------------:|
| 1    | 6, 8         |
| 2    | 5, 9         |
| 3    | 4, 10        |
| 4    | 3, 11        |
| 5    | 2, 12        |
| 6    | 1            |
| 7    | — (instant win) |
| 8    | 1            |
| 9    | 2            |
| 10   | 3            |
| 11   | 4            |
| 12   | 5            |

The connection icons are also printed on the cards for easy reference.

## Team Variant (4 or 6 Players)

For 4 or 6 players, you can play in teams of two. Partners sit across from each other.

### Team Setup

After dealing and sorting hands, each teammate passes **one card** to their partner (who places it in the correct sorted position). No other communication is allowed between partners.

### Team Play

- Play proceeds normally with individual turns.
- Partners **share** their collected trios (place them together in front of one partner).
- Each time an opposing team completes a trio, your team may perform **one swap** — exchange a card with your partner (placed in correct sorted position).
- Win conditions remain the same but apply to the team's combined trios.

## Strategy Tips

- **Track revealed cards** — remember what numbers were shown and returned to specific locations.
- **Watch hand sizes** — if a player has few cards left, you know more about their range.
- **Use yourself** — revealing your own lowest/highest is safe information you already know.
- **In Spicy mode** — focus on connected pairs rather than collecting any three trios.
