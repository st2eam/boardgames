# UNO Rules

## Overview

UNO is a classic card game where players take turns matching a card in their hand with the current card shown on top of the deck.

## Components

- 108 cards total:
  - 19 Blue cards (0-9)
  - 19 Green cards (0-9)
  - 19 Red cards (0-9)
  - 19 Yellow cards (0-9)
  - 8 Draw Two cards (2 of each color)
  - 8 Reverse cards (2 of each color)
  - 8 Skip cards (2 of each color)
  - 4 Wild cards
  - 4 Wild Draw Four cards

## Setup

1. Shuffle the deck.
2. Deal 7 cards to each player.
3. Place the remaining deck face down as the draw pile.
4. Turn over the top card to start the discard pile. If it's a Wild or action card, follow special rules (see below).

## Turn Structure

On your turn, play one card from your hand by matching the top card of the discard pile by:
- **Color**: Match the color
- **Number**: Match the number
- **Symbol**: Match the action symbol

If you can't play, draw one card from the draw pile. You may play that card immediately if it matches, otherwise your turn ends.

Before playing your second-to-last card, you must say "UNO!" If caught not saying UNO, draw 2 penalty cards.

## Action Cards

- **Skip**: Next player loses their turn.
- **Reverse**: Reverses the direction of play.
- **Draw Two (+2)**: Next player draws 2 cards and loses their turn.
- **Wild**: Player chooses the color for the next turn.
- **Wild Draw Four (+4)**: Player chooses the color AND the next player draws 4 cards and loses their turn. Can only be played if you have no cards matching the current color.

## Ending the Game

The first player to play all their cards wins the round. Points are scored:
- Number cards: Face value
- Skip/Reverse/Draw Two: 20 points each
- Wild/Wild Draw Four: 50 points each

First player to 500 points wins the game.
