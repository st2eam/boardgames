# Wingspan: Oceania Expansion Rules

## Overview

The Oceania Expansion is the second expansion for Wingspan, adding 95 new bird cards featuring birds from Australia and New Zealand, a new nectar food type, redesigned player mats, and new yellow "end of game" bird powers. This expansion requires the base game to play.

## Components

- 95 bird cards (Australia/New Zealand birds)
- 5 new player mats (alternate design)
- 5 new food dice (including nectar faces)
- 69 nectar tokens
- 15 yellow egg miniatures
- 5 bonus cards
- 4 new goal tiles
- New scorepad and reference tile
- Automa components

## Setup

Shuffle the expansion bird cards, bonus cards, and goal tiles into the corresponding base game decks. Replace the base game food dice with the new Oceania dice. You may choose to use either the original player mats or the new Oceania mats (nectar requires the Oceania mats and dice).

## New Mechanics

### Nectar

Nectar is the biggest new mechanic:

- **New food type**: Nectar can substitute for any other food type when playing a bird. When spent, place the nectar token on a **"spent nectar" space** on your player mat (in the habitat where the bird was played).
- **Scoring nectar**: At the end of the game, count spent nectar in each habitat separately. The player with the **most nectar in each habitat** earns 5 bonus points; second most earns 3; third earns 1. Unspent nectar is worth nothing.
- **Nectar dice**: The new dice include nectar faces alongside the standard food types. Nectar is wild — it can be used as any food.

### New Player Mats

The Oceania player mats replace the original mats and introduce:

- **Reset birdfeeder / Reset card tray**: As an action (instead of a regular turn action), you can reset the birdfeeder dice or the face-up bird card tray. This costs 1 action cube.
- **Faster early-game resources**: The mats let you gain more food and cards earlier, but laying eggs is reduced compared to the original mats.
- **No-food-cost columns**: The first column in each habitat on the new mats has no food cost for playing a bird, making early plays easier.
- You can **choose** to play with the original mats or Oceania mats, but nectar rules require the Oceania mats and dice.

### Yellow "End of Game" Powers

New yellow bird powers activate **after the game ends** (after final scoring), allowing abilities like:
- Tucking additional cards
- Caching extra food
- Laying extra eggs for bonus scoring

These powers resolve before the final score tally.

### All-Player Interaction Powers

Some new birds have powers that affect all players (e.g., "all players gain 1 food from the supply"), adding more interaction to the game.

## New End-of-Round Goals

The expansion adds new goal tiles including:
- Birds with body parts in their names
- Birds with specific beak directions
- Rounds with no end goal (a new possibility)

## Key Differences from Base Game

| Aspect | Base Game | Oceania |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Food dice | 5 standard dice | 5 dice with nectar faces |
| Player mats | Standard layout | Redesigned with reset actions |
| Early game | Slower buildup | Faster food and card gain |
| Egg strategy | Strong late-game | Reduced egg output |
| Food type | 5 types | 6 types (with nectar) |
